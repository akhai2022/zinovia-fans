# Landing Page — High-Converting Spec

**Product:** Creator subscription platform (Fanvue competitor).  
**Goals:** Creator signups, fan trust, one primary CTA (e.g. “Start as creator” or “Join as fan”), fast scan on mobile.

---

## 1. Section order (top to bottom)

1. **Hero** — Creator value prop + fan trust; 1 primary CTA, 1 secondary CTA  
2. **How it works** — 3 steps (creator or fan path, or both)  
3. **Social proof** — Creator earnings proof style (no fake numbers)  
4. **Feature grid** — Monetization, messaging, privacy, analytics  
5. **Creator testimonials** — Structure only  
6. **Pricing teaser** — Not full pricing page  
7. **Trust & safety + payouts** — Reassurance block  
8. **FAQ** — Billing, privacy, refunds framing  
9. **Footer** — IA + legal + secondary CTAs  

---

## 2. Section layouts & microcopy

### 2.1 Hero

**Purpose:** One clear value prop (creators earn / fans get trusted experience) and two CTAs.

**Layout — Mobile**

- Full viewport or near (min-h ~85vh or ~70vh if nav is sticky).
- **Top:** Sticky nav: Logo, “For creators” / “For fans” (optional tabs or links), “Log in”, primary CTA (e.g. “Get started”).
- **Content (stack, centered):**
  - Headline (H1), one line preferred, max ~8 words.
  - Subhead (1–2 sentences), max ~120 chars visible above fold.
  - Two CTAs: Primary button (filled), Secondary (outline or text).
  - Optional: small trust line under CTAs (“No credit card to browse” / “Free to join”).
- **Visual:** Device mock (phone with feed/profile) or abstract gradient/shape; avoid busy hero image.

**Layout — Desktop**

- Split or centered. Option A: 50/50 (copy left, visual right). Option B: 60/40 copy-led, visual supports.
- Same H1, subhead, CTAs; subhead can be slightly longer (e.g. 140 chars).
- Primary CTA same as mobile; secondary can sit beside or below.

**Microcopy (see Section 4 for options)**

- Headline: pick 1 of 5 options.
- Subhead: pick 1 of 5 options.
- Primary CTA: e.g. “Start as creator” or “Find creators”.
- Secondary CTA: e.g. “See how it works” (anchor to How it works) or “For fans”.

---

### 2.2 How it works (3 steps)

**Purpose:** Reduce friction; “this is simple.”

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “How it works” (or “Simple for creators” / “Simple for everyone”).
- 3 cards or 3 rows, stacked. Each: number (1–3) or icon, short title, 1-line description.
- Optional: single illustration showing flow (sign up → set price → get paid) or (discover → subscribe → enjoy).
- CTA at bottom: “Get started” (same as hero primary) or “See pricing”.

**Layout — Desktop**

- 3 columns, same content; numbers/icons above title; descriptions 1–2 lines.
- Optional: horizontal timeline or connected steps.

**Microcopy (structure)**

- **Step 1:** “Create your space” / “Set your page in minutes. Add a photo, price, and you’re live.”
- **Step 2:** “Share & earn” / “Post content. Subscribers get access; you get paid monthly.”
- **Step 3:** “Get paid on time” / “Payouts to your bank. Transparent fees, no surprises.”

(If fan angle: Discover → Subscribe → Support.)

---

### 2.3 Social proof (creator earnings proof, no fake numbers)

**Purpose:** Credibility without fabricated stats.

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “Creators are growing with [Product]” (or “Built for real creators”).
- **Options (pick one or combine):**
  - **Quotes only:** 1–2 short creator quotes about earning or reliability; no numbers. E.g. “Finally a platform that pays on time.”
  - **Proof style:** “Join creators who’ve already been paid out” + “We’ve processed payouts to creators in 50+ countries” (true, operational fact).
  - **Logos:** “Trusted by creators from [X], [Y], [Z]” (categories or regions, not fake metrics).
- No “$X earned” unless from a single, real, permissioned case study.

**Layout — Desktop**

- Same; can add a second quote or a small “Payouts every month” visual (icon + short line).

**Microcopy examples**

- “Creators get paid. On time, every time.”
- “Join creators who’ve already received payouts.”
- “Built for creators who take their income seriously.”

---

### 2.4 Feature grid (monetization, messaging, privacy, analytics)

**Purpose:** Quick scan of why choose us (creators + fans).

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “Everything you need to grow.”
- 2×2 or 4×1 grid. Each cell: icon, title, 1-line description.
- Four themes: **Monetization** (subscriptions, tips, paid DMs), **Messaging** (DMs, requests), **Privacy** (you control who sees what), **Analytics** (see what’s working).

**Layout — Desktop**

- 2×2 or 4×1; icons left or top; more room for 1–2 lines per feature.

**Microcopy (titles + one line)**

- **Monetization:** “Subscriptions, tips & paid messages” — “One place to earn from your community.”
- **Messaging:** “DMs that respect boundaries” — “Message requests, subscriber-only DMs, you choose.”
- **Privacy:** “You’re in control” — “Decide what’s public, for followers, or for subscribers only.”
- **Analytics:** “See what works” — “Simple stats so you can focus on content that converts.”

---

### 2.5 Creator testimonials (structure only)

**Purpose:** Emotional proof; structure given, copy to be filled with real quotes.

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “What creators say” (or “From the community”).
- 1–2 cards visible; horizontal scroll or stacked. Each card: quote (2–3 lines), avatar, name, handle/category (e.g. “Creator · Fitness”).
- No star ratings unless we have real reviews.

**Layout — Desktop**

- 2–3 columns or a carousel; same card structure.

**Structure**

- Quote (short, specific: payouts, support, ease of use).
- Attribution: Name, optional handle, category/niche.
- Optional: “Verified creator” or “Paid creator” badge (only if we have a verification program).

---

### 2.6 Pricing teaser (not full pricing page)

**Purpose:** Set expectation and drive to signup or dedicated pricing page.

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “Simple pricing” or “One price, your rules.”
- One card or one line: e.g. “You set your subscription price. We handle payments and payouts. Transparent fee.” + CTA “See how pricing works” or “Get started”.
- Or: “From $X/month” (if we have a floor) + “You keep the rest” + CTA.

**Layout — Desktop**

- Same idea; can add a simple comparison line: “No hidden fees. No surprise charges.”

**Microcopy**

- “You set your price. We handle the rest.”
- “Transparent fees. Payouts you can count on.”
- CTA: “See pricing” / “Get started” / “Start earning”.

---

### 2.7 Trust & safety + payouts reassurance

**Purpose:** Reduce anxiety (fans: safe payment; creators: I get paid).

**Layout — Mobile**

- Two short blocks or one block with two parts:
  - **Trust & safety:** “Secure payments” / “Pay with confidence. Card details never shared with creators.” Plus “Report and block anytime.”
  - **Payouts:** “Reliable payouts” / “Bank transfers on a regular schedule. Clear statements, no surprises.”
- Optional: small icons (lock, shield, bank).

**Layout — Desktop**

- Side-by-side or one row with two columns.

**Microcopy**

- “Secure payments. Your card is never shared with creators.”
- “Report and block. We take safety seriously.”
- “Payouts on schedule. Clear fees, clear statements.”

---

### 2.8 FAQ (billing, privacy, refunds framing)

**Purpose:** Answer objections and frame policies clearly.

**Layout — Mobile**

- Section title: “Common questions.”
- Accordions (5–7). One question per row; tap to expand answer (2–4 lines).

**Layout — Desktop**

- Same; can be 2 columns of accordions.

**Suggested questions (framing only; answers to be legal-compliant)**

- “How does subscription billing work?” — Monthly recurring; cancel anytime; card charged each period.
- “Can I cancel anytime?” — Yes; access until period end; no refund for current period (or per policy).
- “Is my payment info safe?” — Processed by [Stripe]; we don’t store full card details; secure checkout.
- “What’s your refund policy?” — Frame: “Refunds are handled per our policy; contact support for eligible cases.”
- “Who can see my content?” — You choose: public, followers, or subscribers only.
- “How do creator payouts work?” — Regular schedule; to bank; fees shown in dashboard.

---

### 2.9 Footer IA

**Structure**

- **Column 1 — Product:** For creators, For fans, How it works, Pricing, Features (or link to feature grid).
- **Column 2 — Company:** About, Blog (if any), Careers, Press.
- **Column 3 — Support:** Help center, Contact, Status.
- **Column 4 — Legal:** Privacy policy, Terms of service, Cookie policy, Refund policy (or link from FAQ).
- **Bottom row:** Logo, “© [Year] [Product]. All rights reserved.” Optional: social icons, app store badges (if applicable).

**Mobile:** Stack columns; optional collapse “Product” / “Company” / “Support” / “Legal” under headings.

## 6. Recommended UI components

| Section        | Components |
|----------------|------------|
| **Hero**       | Nav (logo, 2–3 links, Log in, primary CTA), H1, paragraph, Primary button, Secondary button (outline or text), optional trust line |
| **How it works** | Section heading, 3 cards (number/icon + title + description), optional illustration, CTA button |
| **Social proof** | Section heading, quote block(s) and/or stat line (operational fact), optional logo strip |
| **Feature grid** | Section heading, 4 feature cards (icon + title + short copy) in 2×2 or 4×1 |
| **Testimonials** | Section heading, testimonial cards (quote + avatar + name + category), horizontal scroll or grid |
| **Pricing teaser** | Section heading, one pricing card or one-line value prop, CTA button |
| **Trust & safety** | Two text blocks (icon + heading + 1–2 lines), optional lock/shield/bank icons |
| **FAQ**          | Section heading, accordion list (question + expandable answer) |
| **Footer**      | 4-column link groups (or stacked on mobile), copyright row, optional social / app badges |

**Global**

- Sticky nav with primary CTA.
- Buttons: one filled (primary), one outline or text (secondary).
- Spacing: consistent vertical rhythm (e.g. 4/6/8 spacing units between sections).
- Typography: one clear H1, one subhead style, body for descriptions; avoid more than 3 type styles on the page.

This gives you section order, layout notes, microcopy, and exact headline/subhead/CTA options plus a component map for implementation.
